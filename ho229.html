Early Aerospace Endeavor

At thirteen years old, after being impressed by the visionary aerodynamics of the Horten Ho 229 flying wing, I embarked on one of my first serious aerospace project. With foam core construction and traditional electronic parts, this early radio-controlled aircraft effort provided critical lessons in systems integration and aerodynamics when the prototype had numerous stability and thrust shortcomings.

Technical Challenges and Learning Outcomes

  • Propulsion Limitations: The propulsion system had to be improvised due to the absence of EDF (Electronic Ducted Fan), due to which I had to design my own EDF. This improvised EDF had various issues, the thrust limitations was one of the most significant aspect.
  • Aerodynamic Instability: The aircraft had a lot of structural and aerodynamic instabilities. Primarily, the aircraft was not aerodynamic due to its bulkiness.
  • Systems Integration: Early lessons in weight distribution

Applied Knowledge

These formative experiences directly influenced my later work in designing UAVs and other experimental RC works. The R.A.3.D. stealth UAV program utilized lessons related to:

  • Weight distribution optimization
  • Power system dependability
  • Aerodynamic polishing by iterative testing

Professional Reflection

This development from rudimentary prototypes to complex systems illustrates my love of aerospace engineering : every iteration, both successful and unsuccessful, generates information to drive the next development cycle. The practice of systematic testing and adjustment continues refine my approach to aerospace engineering.
